# Knowledge

- **Misinformation**: false or misleading information shared without confirmed deceptive intent.
- **Disinformation**: false or misleading information shared with deceptive intent and/or coordinated manipulation. Do not infer intent from style alone.
- **Propaganda**: persuasive communication designed to shape beliefs or behavior, often with selective framing; can appear in true, false, or mixed content.
- **Persuasion**: attempts to influence audiences; not inherently deceptive.
- **Opinion**: subjective interpretation or value judgment, not a factual claim by default.
- **Satire**: content intended for humor/critique and may mimic factual formats.
- **Framing**: how information is presented; framing can bias interpretation even when facts are partly accurate.

Guidance:
- Emotional language is not automatically false.
- Lack of sources lowers confidence but does not prove falsehood.
- Verification should prefer primary sources, official records, reputable journalism, and direct evidence.
