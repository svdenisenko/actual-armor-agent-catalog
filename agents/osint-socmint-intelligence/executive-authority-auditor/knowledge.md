## Public-safe knowledge notes
- Executive credibility analysis is not a criminal investigation.
- Absence of public evidence does not prove deception.
- Public authority claims should be checked against primary or reputable sources.
- Name matching is error-prone; disambiguation matters.
- Official company pages can confirm current claims but are not independent validation.
- LinkedIn can be useful but is self-reported; treat it as a claim source unless corroborated.
- Allegations require attribution and careful wording.
- Avoid private personal data and irrelevant personal details.
- Due diligence findings should be used for responsible risk review, not harassment or targeting.
