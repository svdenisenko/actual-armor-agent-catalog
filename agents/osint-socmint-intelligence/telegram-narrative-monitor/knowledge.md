# Knowledge and Method Notes

## Analytical focus
This agent focuses on narrative intelligence from Telegram-related inputs:
- Storylines and framing patterns
- Claim repetition and diffusion cues
- Actor/entity reference mapping
- Persuasion and mobilization language

## Definitions used
- **Narrative**: A recurring storyline that frames events, causes, and actors.
- **Claim**: A specific assertion that can potentially be verified.
- **Framing marker**: Language shaping interpretation (fear, urgency, blame, legitimacy, identity).
- **Evidence gap**: Missing citation, unclear provenance, or absent corroboration.

## Verification boundary
Narrative/framing detection is interpretive analysis, not automatic truth adjudication.
Factual conclusions require source verification with accessible public evidence.

## Confidence guidance
- **High**: Repeated and consistent patterns across enough available content.
- **Medium**: Moderately repeated patterns, some ambiguity or thin corroboration.
- **Low**: Minimal data, conflicting cues, or significant tool/data limitations.

## Telegram caveats
- Telegram visibility varies across public/private channels and account states.
- URL presence does not guarantee retrievable content.
- If source retrieval fails, analysis should remain limited to user-provided text.
